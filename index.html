A Feast of Blood

A Feast of Blood is our family’s collaborative universe — an original Targaryen tale set between Aegon’s Conquest and House of the Dragon. A lost story imagined from our own roleplay, where we bring new characters and legends to the world of A Song of Ice and Fire.

“In the shadow of the Conquest, before the Dance, the forgotten rise.”

Houses of Our Lineage

House Targaryen

During King Gaerys Targaryen’s reign, House Targaryen remained the ruling dynasty of the Seven Kingdoms. Dragons still lived, though fewer in number, and the royal seat on Dragonstone retained its ancient Valyrian customs. Despite outward strength, the House was marked by internal strife, political unrest, and growing discontent among the nobility. Gaerys's harsh rule deepened divisions within the realm and the royal family, setting the stage for future conflict.

House Lannister

In the time of King Gaerys, House Lannister remained one of the richest and most powerful Great Houses, ruling the Westerlands from Casterly Rock. However, their failed rebellion against the crown led to a sharp decline in influence. Their pride wounded and their name publicly shamed, the Lannisters maintained control over their region but were closely watched by the Iron Throne.

Read More ➔

House Stark

House Stark ruled the North from Winterfell with customary independence and reserve. While loyal to the crown in name, they remained largely removed from southern politics. The harsh climate and ancient traditions of the North kept the Starks focused on local governance, defense, and maintaining the old ways, rather than engaging in courtly intrigue.

Read More ➔

House Tyrell

In the sun-drenched fields of the Reach, House Tyrell bloomed with beauty, courtesy, and ambition—but beneath the blossoms, thorns grew sharp. Under the reign of King Gaerys Targaryen, the Reach remained fertile and rich, but its stewards at Highgarden learned to tread carefully amid the shadows cast by the Iron Throne.

Read More ➔

House Baratheon

From the storm-lashed cliffs of Storm's End, House Baratheon stood defiant as ever—unyielding, thunder-hearted, and fiercely proud of its bloodline. Though bound by oaths to House Targaryen since Orys Baratheon’s conquest, the Storm Lords did not wear their loyalty lightly. Under King Gaerys Targaryen, a ruler whose iron grip tightened with paranoia and cruelty, the ancient tempers of the Baratheons began to stir.

Read More ➔

House Martell

Far to the south, beyond the sands and scorn of the other kingdoms, House Martell of Sunspear remained proud, unbent, and deliberately distant from the chaos that festered in King’s Landing. Though nominally sworn to the Iron Throne since Dorne’s peaceful unification with the realm, the Princes and Princesses of Dorne had always played a different game—slow, silent, and steeped in generational memory.

Read More ➔

House Arryn

Perched high in the Eyrie, among snow and sky, House Arryn watched the realm from afar—as distant, cold, and aloof as the mountains they ruled. Known as the Blood of Andals, the Arryns had always seen themselves as a purer nobility than the dragonlords who came from across the sea. In the era of King Gaerys Targaryen, that quiet disdain deepened into something more dangerous: guarded neutrality.

Read More ➔

House Tully

In the green heart of the Riverlands, where the rivers Red, Tumblestone, and Trident converge, House Tully of Riverrun upheld its words—"Family, Duty, Honor"—even as the realm twisted under the weight of a cruel king. Under King Gaerys Targaryen, those three words became a quiet act of defiance.

Read More ➔

House Greyjoy

On the bleak and battered rocks of Pyke, where the sea never sleeps and the winds shriek like ghosts, House Greyjoy endured—untamed, unwelcome, and proud. To the Iron Throne, they were vassals. To themselves, they were kings.

Read More ➔

House Hightower

From the towering heights of the Hightower in Oldtown, where flame and fog coil around ancient stone and whispered knowledge, House Hightower watched the realm crumble beneath the weight of dragonfire and madness. They did not move hastily, nor speak often, but their silence was not absence—it was calculation.
